## Design Context

### Users
This is a personal portfolio for Marco Bértolo, aimed at recruiters, hiring managers, and collaborators evaluating his fit for software development roles. Visitors need a fast, credible overview of his profile, technical range, practical experience, education, and contact details.

### Brand Personality
Focused, ambitious, grounded. The interface should feel sharp, contemporary, and confident without looking corporate or generic. It should communicate technical seriousness, growth potential, and professional maturity.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial portfolio with a cinematic hero, strong typographic contrast, warm light surfaces, dark ink tones, and copper-green accents. The look should avoid startup-dashboard cliches, glassmorphism overload, and generic template cards. Light mode is the primary presentation.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with credibility: show role, location, studies, and strengths immediately.
2. Use hierarchy, not clutter: every section should clarify a different part of the profile.
3. Make technical breadth feel curated: group skills, projects, and experience into purposeful blocks.
4. Keep motion restrained and meaningful: subtle reveals and hover response, never decoration for its own sake.
5. Design for scanning: recruiters should grasp the story in under a minute on desktop or mobile.
